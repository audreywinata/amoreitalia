BOOK WITH AMORE

Rome

Rome, the capital of Italy, combines rich ancient history with modern charm. Famous for landmarks like the Colosseum, Spanish Steps, and the Vatican, it offers world-class museums and historic sites. Beyond the tourist spots, Rome also has lively local areas that reflect its vibrant spirit.

Venice

Venice is a uniquely romantic city built on over 100 islands linked by canals and bridges. Known for its grand palaces, historic churches, and rich museums, its car-free streets and winding alleys hide countless treasures, making it a place full of charm and endless discovery.

Florence

Florence, the capital of Tuscany, is the birthplace of the Renaissance and rich in art, architecture, and history. Shaped by the Medici family's legacy, the city features iconic sites like Michelangelo’s David, the Duomo, and masterpieces by Botticelli and Da Vinci. It's a must-visit for art and history lovers.

Milan

Milan, Italy’s financial and fashion capital, blends modern energy with historic elegance. Known for the stunning Duomo, the city offers diverse experiences—from La Scala opera and world-class shopping to charming cafés and iconic art. Milan is a dynamic and essential destination in any Italian journey.

Trieste

Trieste, a historic port city and former Habsburg territory, is a cultural crossroads shaped by Italian, German, Croatian, and Greek influences. Now the capital of Friuli-Venezia Giulia, it’s known for its rich café culture and has the highest coffee consumption per capita in Italy.

Capri

The island of Capri, in the Bay of Naples, is a popular destination known for its famous grottos. A true Capri experience, however, goes beyond the port, with open-air taxis taking visitors up scenic hills to the chic town of Capri or the peaceful Anacapri, offering a taste of the island's charm and natural beauty.

Naples

Naples is a vibrant city where ancient history meets raw energy. Founded by the Greeks as Neapolis, it sits beneath Mount Vesuvius and reflects a rich cultural mix. Often skipped for nearby Pompeii, Naples rewards those who explore its lively streets and try its famous Neapolitan pizza.

Bologna

Bologna, the capital of Emilia Romagna, is a city where rich history meets world-famous cuisine. Known for Europe’s oldest university and its role in Italy’s “food valley,” it offers a unique blend of intellectual heritage and culinary excellence, making it a rewarding destination for both culture and food lovers.

Verona

Verona, a small city in northeastern Italy, gained international fame through Shakespeare's The Two Gentlemen of Verona and Romeo & Juliet. The legacy of the tragic love story attracts romantics worldwide, with Juliet's iconic balcony being the city's top attraction.

Matera

Matera, located in southern Italy, is renowned for the Sassi, its ancient cave dwellings carved into rock. Once one of Italy’s poorest areas, it has undergone a remarkable transformation and now stands as a fascinating and historic city, drawing visitors with its unique architecture and deep sense of history.

Aeolian Islands

Sicily’s Aeolian Islands, an archipelago of volcanic islands off the northeastern shore, offers hidden gems. Lipari, the largest and busiest island, serves as a convenient base, while Salina provides a more peaceful, relaxing escape for those seeking tranquility.

Sicily

Sicily, the Mediterranean’s largest island, offers incredible diversity in landscape, culture, and cuisine. From snow-capped Mount Etna to coastal towns like Palermo, each region tells its own story. With UNESCO sites, traditional foods, and strong local pride, Sicily feels more like a continent than an island.
